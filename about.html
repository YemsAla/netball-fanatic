A brief history

Netball is a fast-paced team sport that originated from basketball but with its own unique rules and gameplay. It's typically played by two teams of seven players each, aiming to score goals by shooting the ball through a hoop at each end of the court. Netball has a fascinating history, dating back to the late 19th century when it was first played in England as an adapted version of basketball. Over time, netball evolved into its own distinct sport, with organized leagues and competitions established across the UK and Commonwealth countries. The game is played on a rectangular court with designated areas for each player position. Players can't move with the ball and must pass it to teammates or shoot for goal within a certain time frame. The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins. Netball is known for its emphasis on teamwork, agility, and strategic thinking. Each player has a specific role on the court, such as shooter, defender, or center, requiring them to work together to outmaneuver the opposing team. Overall, netball is a thrilling and dynamic sport that combines elements of skill, strategy, and teamwork, making it a popular choice for players of all ages and abilities worldwide.

Rules of the game

  • Netball is played by two teams of seven players each.
  • Players are assigned specific positions, including shooter, goal attack, wing attack, center, wing defence, goal defense and goalkeeper.
  • The objective is to score goals by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop, which is positioned at each end of the court.
  • Players are not allowed to run with the ball or dribble; instead, they must pass it to teammates using only their hands.
  • Once a player receives the ball, they must pass it or shoot for goal within 3 seconds. Failure to do so results in losing possession of the ball to the other team
  • Players are not allowed to make physical contact with opponents, and defensive players must stay at least 0.9 meters away from the player with the ball.
  • Each game consists of four quarters, each lasting 15 minutes, with a short break between quarters and a longer halftime break.
  • The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins.
  • Umpires officiate the game and enforce the rules to ensure fair play and safety for all players.
